How does the state define telemedicine?    
“Telehealth” means the delivery of services from a provider of health care to a patient at a different location through the use of information and audio-visual communication technology, not including standard telephone, facsimile or electronic mail. [1],[7].

 

Practice Standards and Licensure

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Must a provider establish a relationship with a patient prior to engaging with them via telemedicine? No [8] Exception: Osteopathic physicians must establish a relationship with patients before providing services via telemedicine.
Must a telepresenter or health care provider be present with a patient during a telemedicine encounter? No [7]
Must providers obtain informed consent from a patient prior to a telemedicine encounter? No [8] Exception: Osteopathic physicians must obtain unspecified form of consent.
If applicable, what type of consent is required? NA [8]  
Can telemedicine providers from bordering states legally conduct telemedicine visits with patients in this state? Yes [5] As long as a health care provider is licensed in Nevada—and therefore subject to State laws and regulations— he or she can provide telehealth services to people in Nevada, regardless of the provider’s location.
Has this state enacted laws adopting the Federal State Medical Boards (FSMB) compact which enforces expedited licensing for out-of-state practices? Yes [2]  
 

Medicaid Coverage and Reimbursement

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Is there state legislation regarding Medicaid reimbursement for telemedicine services? Yes [1]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for services rendered via telemedicine? Yes [7]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse telemedicine services at the same rate as services delivered in-person? Yes [7]  

Medicaid Service Limitations

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state Medicaid program apply any geographic restrictions to telemedicine encounters? Yes [7] The originating site must be located within the state. The provider of health care and patient must be in different locations. In order to receive coverage for a telehealth facility fee, the originating site must be an enrolled Medicaid provider. If a patient is receiving telehealth services at an originating site not enrolled in Medicaid, the originating site is not eligible for a facility fee from the DHCFP.
Does the state Medicaid program limit coverage and reimbursement for telemedicine to a prescribed set of health conditions or services? No [7]

Medicaid manual states: Services provided via telehealth must be clinically appropriate and within the health care professional's scope of practice as established by its licensing agency.

A NV Medicaid representative confirmed that most services that comply with Medicaid policies and can be delivered via telehealth (e.g., non-invasive procedures, etc.) would be approved.

Exceptions: The following services must be provided in-person and are not considered appropriate services to be provided via telehealth: Home Health Services provided by a Registered Nurse (RN), Physical Therapist (PT), Occupational Therapist, Speech Therapist, Respiratory Therapist, Dietician or Home Health Aide as identified in provider qualifications found in MSM Chapter 1400, Home Health Agency (HHA)

Does the state Medicaid program limit the frequency with which they will reimburse for telemedicine services for a given patient? No [7]
Does the state Medicaid plan restrict or provide exceptions for services provided via telemedicine for end-stage renal disease or dialysis? (e.g., in-person check-ups between treatments) Yes

1. ESRD visits must include at least one in-person visit to examine the vascular access site by a provider; however, an interactive audio/video telecommunications system may be used for providing additional visits.

2. Medical records must indicate that at least one of the visits was furnished in-person by a provider. Refer to MSM Chapter 600, Physician Services, for medical coverage requirements.

Medicaid-Eligible Patient Settings

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state limit Medicaid reimbursement for telemedicine services based on patient setting (e.g., where the patient is located at the time of a telemedicine encounter)? No [7] "The originating site must be located within the state."

Medicaid-Eligible Provider Settings

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state limit Medicaid reimbursement for telemedicine services based on the type of provider facilitating the telemedicine encounter? No [3] The distant site provider must be an enrolled Medicaid provider.

Medicaid-Eligible Technologies

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via live video conference? Yes[7]
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via store and forward technologies? Yes [7] Note: These services are not eligible for originating site facility fees.
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via remote patient monitoring? Yes [5]
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via email/phone/fax? No [7]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for synchronous telemedicine encounters? Yes [7]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for asynchronous telemedicine encounters? Yes [7] Note: These services are not eligible for originating site facility fees.
 

Corporate Practice of Medicine

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Do state laws or regulations prohibit the corporate practice of medicine? Yes [5] Not prohibited by statute, but prohibition has been upheld by court opinions.

Details on innovative payment and service delivery models or other state programs that include telehealth.    
Home and Community Based Assisted Living Waiver - Nevada Medicaid's Home and Community Based Waiver Program helps older state residents avoid institutional (nursing home) placement by offering a range of support services that help them to remain living in their homes or in the homes of family members. Health care, medical care, and nursing services are not included benefits of this waiver, as it is intended to provide non-medical care services only. Instead, services such as personal care (at home or in approved state assisted living communities), adult day care, and chore services help families care for loved ones at home or help frail, elderly persons maintain their independence. [9].
Clarifying Information:
Telehealth services provided to this waiver population are reimbursed by Medicaid per Medicaid Manual Chapter 3400
