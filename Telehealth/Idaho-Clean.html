How does the state define telemedicine?    
"Telehealth services" means health care services provided by a provider to a person through the use of electronic communications, information technology, asynchronous store and forward transfer or synchronous interaction between a provider at a distant site and a patient at an originating site.[6]

 

Practice Standards and Licensure

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Must a provider establish a relationship with a patient prior to engaging with them via telemedicine? No[6] Provider-patient relationship can be established through telemedicine.
Must a telepresenter or health care provider be present with a patient during a telemedicine encounter? No  
Must providers obtain informed consent from a patient prior to a telemedicine encounter? Yes[6][3]  
If applicable, what type of consent is required? Written[6][3]  
Can telemedicine providers from bordering states legally conduct telemedicine visits with patients in this state? No[3] The distant provider must maintain a current ID license. There is no explicit mention or restriction on whether the distant provider can be out-of-state.
Has this state enacted laws adopting the Federal State Medical Boards (FSMB) compact which enforces expedited licensing for out-of-state practices? Yes[7] Status: IMLC Member State serving as SPL processing applications and issuing licenses
 

Medicaid Coverage and Reimbursement

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Is there state legislation regarding Medicaid reimbursement for telemedicine services? No[2] The state legislation on telehealth (ID HB 189) does not address Medicaid reimbursement.
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for services rendered via telemedicine? Yes[3][5] Telehealth services billed without being identified as such are not covered. Services delivered via telehealth may be reimbursed within limitations defined by the Department in the Idaho Medicaid Provider Handbook.
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse telemedicine services at the same rate as services delivered in-person? Yes[1] Idaho Medicaid reimbursement is the same for services delivered via telehealth and services delivered face-to-face in the community based fee-for-service methodology.

Medicaid Service Limitations

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state Medicaid program apply any geographic restrictions to telemedicine encounters? No Reference Found  
Does the state Medicaid program limit coverage and reimbursement for telemedicine to a prescribed set of health conditions or services? Yes[3][4] services include:
  • Physician/Non-physician Practitioner Services - provider must be a physician or non-physician practitioner:
    • Primary Care Services
    • Specialty Services
    • Psychotherapy with evaluation and management
    • Psychiatric diagnostic interview
    • Pharmacological management
  • Therapeutic Consultation and Crisis Intervention - for children with developmental disabilities; provider must be a Developmental Disabilities Agency, an independent Medicaid provider under agreement with the Department, or under the Infant Toddler Program; provider must have a Doctoral or Master’s degree in psychology, education, applied behavioral analysis, or in a related discipline with 1500 hours of relevant coursework or training, or both, in principles of child development, learning theory, positive behavior support techniques, dual diagnosis, or behavior analysis, and two years relevant experience in designing and implementing comprehensive behavioral therapies for children with DD and challenging behavior
  • Therapy Services - provided by a licensed speech language pathologist, occupational therapists, or physical therapists; does not cover evaluations
  • School-based Community Based Rehabilitation Services (CBRS) Supervision in the Educational Environment
  • Interpretive Services - includes language/oral or sign language
Does the state Medicaid program limit the frequency with which they will reimburse for telemedicine services for a given patient? No Reference Found  
Does the state Medicaid plan restrict or provide exceptions for services provided via telemedicine for end-stage renal disease or dialysis? (e.g., in-person check-ups between treatments) No Reference Found  

Medicaid-Eligible Patient Settings

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state limit Medicaid reimbursement for telemedicine services based on patient setting (e.g., where the patient is located at the time of a telemedicine encounter)? No[3] No explicit limitations mentioned regarding eligible patient settings.

Medicaid-Eligible Provider Type

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state limit Medicaid reimbursement for telemedicine services based on the type of provider facilitating the telemedicine encounter? Yes[5][3] Eligible providers include:
  • Physicians
  • Physician Assistants
  • Nurse Practitioners
  • Certified Nurse Midwives
  • Clinical Nurse Specialists
  • Marriage and Family Therapists
  • Clinical Social Workers
  • Clinical Counselors
  • Speech Language Pathologists and Audiologists
  • Occupational Therapists
  • Physical Therapists
Note: Only physicians may bill for psychiatric telehealth services.

Medicaid-Eligible Technologies

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via live video conference? Yes[3]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via store and forward technologies? No[3] Technical requirements limit technologies to real-time, two-way audio and video transmission.
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via remote patient monitoring? No[3] Technical requirements limit technologies to real-time, two-way audio and video transmission.
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via email/phone/fax? No[3]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for synchronous telemedicine encounters? Yes[3]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for asynchronous telemedicine encounters? No[3] Technical requirements limit technologies to real-time, two-way audio and video transmission.
 

Corporate Practice of Medicine

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Do state laws or regulations prohibit the corporate practice of medicine? No[8][9] In March 2016, the ID Board of Medicine formally disavowed and rejected the CPOM doctrine.
