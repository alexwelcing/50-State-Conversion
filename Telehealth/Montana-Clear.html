How does the state define telemedicine?    

(a) Telemedicine"" means the use of interactive audio, video, or other telecommunications technology that is: [4]
(1) used by a health care provider or health care facility to deliver health care services at a site other than the site where the patient is located; and
(2) delivered over a secure connection that complies with the requirements of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996, 42 U.S.C. 1320d, et seq.
(ii) The term includes the use of electronic media for consultation relating to the health care diagnosis or treatment of a patient in real time or through the use of store-and-forward technology.
(iii) The term does not include the use of audio-only telephone, e-mail, or facsimile transmissions.
(b) Telehealth"" means the practice of physical therapy using interactive electronic communications, information technology, or other means between a physical therapist in one location and a patient in another location with or without an intervening health care provider.[6]

Clarifying Information:
The telehealth legislation (b) suggests that the term is intended to specifically pertain to physical therapy. However, in various communications (e.g., Notice from Montana Medicaid), the terms "telemedicine" and "telehealth" appear to be used interchangeably.

 

Practice Standards and Licensure

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Must a provider establish a relationship with a patient prior to engaging with them via telemedicine? No[3] Not required, but the Medicaid Manual states "members should establish relationships with primary care providers who are available on a face-to-face basis."
Must a telepresenter or health care provider be present with a patient during a telemedicine encounter? No[4]
Must providers obtain informed consent from a patient prior to a telemedicine encounter? No Reference Found  
If applicable, what type of consent is required? NA  
Can telemedicine providers from bordering states legally conduct telemedicine visits with patients in this state? No[7] Providers treating MT Medicaid members must be licensed in MT.
Has this state enacted laws adopting the Federal State Medical Boards (FSMB) compact which enforces expedited licensing for out-of-state practices? Yes[2]  
 

Medicaid Coverage and Reimbursement

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Is there state legislation regarding Medicaid reimbursement for telemedicine services? Yes[5]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for services rendered via telemedicine? Yes[5]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse telemedicine services at the same rate as services delivered in-person? Yes[5]  

Medicaid Service Limitations

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state Medicaid program apply any geographic restrictions to telemedicine encounters? Yes[3] "The originating and distant providers may not be within the same facility or community." The section does not clarify the definition of a "community."
Does the state Medicaid program limit coverage and reimbursement for telemedicine to a prescribed set of health conditions or services? No[3]  
Does the state Medicaid program limit the frequency with which they will reimburse for telemedicine services for a given patient? No Reference Found[3]
Does the state Medicaid plan restrict or provide exceptions for services provided via telemedicine for end-stage renal disease or dialysis? (e.g., in-person check-ups between treatments) No Reference Found[3]  

Medicaid-Eligible Patient Settings

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state limit Medicaid reimbursement for telemedicine services based on patient setting (e.g., where the patient is located at the time of a telemedicine encounter)? Yes[3] Eligible patient settings:
  • Outpatient Hospital
  • Critical Access Hospital
  • Federally Qualified Health Center
  • Rural Health Center
  • Indian Health Service
  • Mental Health Center
  • Chemical Dependency Clinic
  • Group/Clinic
  • Public Health Clinic
  • Family Planning Clinic or
  • Home

Medicaid-Eligible Provider Type

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state limit Medicaid reimbursement for telemedicine services based on the type of provider facilitating the telemedicine encounter? Yes [3] Eligible providers:
  • Physician
  • Psychiatrist
  • Mid-Levels
  • Dieticians
  • Psychologists
  • Licensed Clinical Social Worker
  • Licensed Professional Counselor

Medicaid-Eligible Technologies

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via live video conference? Yes[4]
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via store and forward technologies? Yes[4]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via remote patient monitoring? No Reference Found [4]
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via email/phone/fax? No reference found [4]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for synchronous telemedicine encounters? Yes[4]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for asynchronous telemedicine encounters? Yes[4]
 

Corporate Practice of Medicine

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Do state laws or regulations prohibit the corporate practice of medicine? Yes[1] Rules of the Montana Board of Medical Examiners provide that it is unprofessional conduct for a physician: “except as provided in this subsection, practicing medicine as the partner, agent, or employee of, or in joint venture with, a person who does not hold a license to practice medicine within this state; however, this does not prohibit: (i) the incorporation of an individual licensee or group of licensees as a professional service corporation under Title 35, chapter 4, MCA.
