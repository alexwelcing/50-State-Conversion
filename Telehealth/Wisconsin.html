How does the state define telemedicine?    

Medicaid: Telehealth enables a provider who is located at a distant site to render the service remotely to a member located at an originating site using a combination of interactive video, audio, and externally acquired images through a networking environment

State Legislature: "Telehealth" is a service provided from a remote location using a combination of interactive video, audio, and externally acquired images through a networking environment between an individual at an originating site and a provider at a remote location with the service being of sufficient audio and visual fidelity and clarity as to be functionally equivalent to face-to-face contact.

"Telehealth" does not include telephone conversations or Internet-based communications between providers or between providers and individuals.

Telemedicine means the practice of medicine when patient care, treatment or services are provided through the use of medical information exchanged from one site to another via electronic communications. Telemedicine does not include the provision of health care services only through an audio only telephone, email messages, text messages, facsimile transmission, mail or parcel service or any combination thereof."[1][2][4]

Practice Standards and Licensure

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Must a provider establish a relationship with a patient No[1][2]  
Must a telepresenter or health care provider be present with a patient during a telemedicine encounter? No Reference Found[1][2][4]  
Must providers obtain informed consent from a patient prior to a telemedicine encounter? Yes[2]  
If applicable, what type of consent is required? Not Sure[2] Type of consent required not specified.
Can telemedicine providers from bordering states legally conduct telemedicine visits with patients in this state? Yes[2] Prior authorization required for out-of-state/bordering state providers.
Exception:
Out-of-state nursing homes not eligible for border status.
Has this state enacted laws adopting the Federal State Medical Boards (FSMB) compact which enforces expedited licensing for out-of-state practices? Yes[3]  
 

Medicaid Coverage and Reimbursement

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Is there state legislation regarding Medicaid reimbursement for telemedicine services? Yes with limitations[4] Legislation is exclusive to mental health services only.
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for services rendered via telemedicine? Yes[2]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse telemedicine services at the same rate as services delivered in-person? Yes[2]  

Medicaid Service Limitations

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state Medicaid program apply any geographic restrictions to telemedicine encounters? No[1][2][4]  
Does the state Medicaid program limit coverage and reimbursement for telemedicine to a prescribed set of health conditions or services? Yes[2] Eligible services include:
  • Adult Mental Health Day Treatment Services
  • Audiology Services
  • Child/Adolescent Day Treatment Services
  • Community Support Program Services
  • Comprehensive Community Services
  • Crisis Intervention Services
  • End-Stage Renal Disease-Related Services
  • Health and Behavior
  • Assessment/Intervention
  • Initial Inpatient Consultations
  • Office or Other Outpatient Services
  • Office or Other Outpatient Consultations
  • Outpatient Mental Health Services (Evaluation,Psychotherapy)
  • Outpatient Substance Abuse Services
  • Substance Abuse Day Treatment
Does the state Medicaid program limit the frequency with which they will reimburse for telemedicine services for a given patient? No[1][2][4]  
Does the state Medicaid plan restrict or provide exceptions for services provided via telemedicine for end-stage renal disease or dialysis? (e.g., in-person check-ups between treatments) No Reference Found[1][2][4] No restrictions found, however end-stage renal disease related services are identified as a covered telemedicine service within the Medicaid program.

Medicaid-Eligible Patient Settings

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state limit Medicaid reimbursement for telemedicine services based on patient setting (e.g., where the patient is located at the time of a telemedicine encounter)? Yes[2] Eligible patient settings/originating sites include:
  • Hospitals, including Emergency Departments
  • Doctor's Office/Clinic
  • Skilled Nursing Facility

Medicaid-Eligible Provider Type

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state limit Medicaid reimbursement for telemedicine services based on the type of provider facilitating the telemedicine encounter? Yes[2] The following types of providers may be reimbursed for providing telehealth services at a distant site:
  • Audiologists (effective for DOS on and after September 1, 2017)
  • Speech language pathologists
  • Nurse midwives
  • Nurse practitioners
  • Ph.D. psychologists in private practice
  • Physician assistants
  • Physicians
  • Psychiatrists
  • Professionals providing services in mental health or substance abuse programs certified by the Division of Quality Assurance (DQA)

Medicaid-Eligible Technologies

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via live video conference? Yes[2]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via store and forward technologies? No[2]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via remote patient monitoring? No[2]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via email/phone/fax? No[2]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for synchronous telemedicine encounters? Yes[2]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for asynchronous telemedicine encounters? No[2]

Do state laws or regulations prohibit the corporate practice of medicine? Yes[5]
