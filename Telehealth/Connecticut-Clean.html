How does the state define telemedicine?    
“Telemedicine” means the use of interactive audio, interactive video or interactive data communication in the delivery of medical advice, diagnosis, care or treatment, and includes the types of services described in subsection (d) of section 20-9 and 42 CFR 410.78(a)(3).
"Telehealth" means the mode of delivering health care or other health services via information and communication technologies to facilitate the diagnosis, consultation and treatment, education, care management and self-management of a patient's physical and mental health, and includes (A) interaction between the patient at the originating site and the telehealth provider at a distant site, and (B) synchronous interactions, asynchronous store and forward transfers or remote patient monitoring. Telehealth does not include the use of facsimile, audio-only telephone, texting or electronic mail. [1][2]

 

Practice Standards and Licensure

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Must a provider establish a relationship with a patient prior to engaging with them via telemedicine? No[2]
Must a telepresenter or health care provider be present with a patient during a telemedicine encounter? No[2]
Must providers obtain informed consent from a patient prior to a telemedicine encounter? Yes[2]
If applicable, what type of consent is required? Not Sure[2] Type of consent is unspecified.
Can telemedicine providers from bordering states legally conduct telemedicine visits with patients in this state? Yes[3] Department of Public Health may establish a process of accepting an applicant’s license from another state and may issue that applicant a license to practice medicine in the state without examination, if certain conditions are met.
Has this state enacted laws adopting the Federal State Medical Boards (FSMB) compact which enforces expedited licensing for out-of-state practices? No[4]
 

Medicaid Coverage and Reimbursement

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Is there state legislation regarding Medicaid reimbursement for telemedicine services? Yes[5]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for services rendered via telemedicine? Yes[5]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse telemedicine services at the same rate as services delivered in-person? No[6] There is currently legislation (HB 5299, 2018) being reviewed by the CT General Assembly that would implement payment parity for telepsychiatry services.

Medicaid Service Limitations

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state Medicaid program apply any geographic restrictions to telemedicine encounters? No
Does the state Medicaid program limit coverage and reimbursement for telemedicine to a prescribed set of health conditions or services? No[5]
Does the state Medicaid program limit the frequency with which they will reimburse for telemedicine services for a given patient? No[2]
Does the state Medicaid plan restrict or provide exceptions for services provided via telemedicine for end-stage renal disease or dialysis? (e.g., in-person check-ups between treatments) No[2]

Medicaid-Eligible Patient Settings

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state limit Medicaid reimbursement for telemedicine services based on patient setting (e.g., where the patient is located at the time of a telemedicine encounter)? No[2]

Medicaid-Eligible Provider Types

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state limit Medicaid reimbursement for telemedicine services based on the type of provider facilitating the telemedicine encounter? Yes[2] "State law restricts telemedicine services to the provider types: physician, physical therapist, chiropractor, naturopath, podiatrist, occupational therapist, optometrist, advanced practice registered nurse, physician assistant, psychologist, marital and family therapist, clinical social worker or master social worker, alcohol and drug counselor, professional counselor, or dietitian-nutritionist."

Medicaid-Eligible Technologies

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via live video conference? Yes[2]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via store and forward technologies? Yes[2]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via remote patient monitoring? Yes[2]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via email/phone/fax? No[2]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for synchronous telemedicine encounters? Yes[2]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for asynchronous telemedicine encounters? Yes[2]  
 

Corporate Practice of Medicine

Question Answer Clarifying Information
