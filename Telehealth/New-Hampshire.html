How does the state define telemedicine?    
Law: "Telemedicine,'' as it pertains to the delivery of health care services, means the use of audio, video, or other electronic media for the purpose of diagnosis, consultation, or treatment. Telemedicine does not include the use of audio-only telephone or facsimile.
Medicaid: In this section, “telehealth services” shall comply with 42 C.F.R. section 410.78. The use of the term “telemedicine” shall comply with the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services requirements governing the aforementioned telehealth services. [1][2][7].

Must a provider establish a relationship with a patient prior to engaging with them via telemedicine? No [4] Special restrictions for licensees who prescribe for a patient whom the licensee does not have a physician-patient relationship.
Must a telepresenter or health care provider be present with a patient during a telemedicine encounter? No [1]
Must providers obtain informed consent from a patient prior to a telemedicine encounter? No [1]
If applicable, what type of consent is required? NA [1]  
Can telemedicine providers from bordering states legally conduct telemedicine visits with patients in this state? Yes [1] The physician or practitioner at the distant site must be licensed to furnish the service under State law.
Has this state enacted laws adopting the Federal State Medical Boards (FSMB) compact which enforces expedited licensing for out-of-state practices? Yes [5]

Medicaid Service Limitations

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state Medicaid program apply any geographic restrictions to telemedicine encounters? Yes [1] All eligible originating sites must be located in either rural professional shortage areas or outside the Metropolitan Statistical Area.
Does the state Medicaid program limit coverage and reimbursement for telemedicine to a prescribed set of health conditions or services? No Reference Found [1]
Does the state Medicaid program limit the frequency with which they will reimburse for telemedicine services for a given patient? Yes[1] [2]

Medicaid policy complies with the Medicare provision described in 42 CFR 410.78

Subsequent hospital care services: 1 telehealth visit every 3 days.

Nursing facility care services: 1 telehealth visit every 30 days.
Does the state Medicaid plan restrict or provide exceptions for services provided via telemedicine for end-stage renal disease or dialysis? (e.g., in-person check-ups between treatments) Yes [1] One "hands-on" visit per month is required for end-stage renal disease-related services to examine the access site.

Medicaid-Eligible Patient Settings

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state limit Medicaid reimbursement for telemedicine services based on patient setting (e.g., where the patient is located at the time of a telemedicine encounter)? Yes [1]

The services are furnished to a beneficiary at an originating site, which is one of the following:

(i) The office of a physician or practitioner.
(ii) A critical access hospital (as described in section 1861(mm)(1) of the Act).
(iii) A rural health clinic (as described in section 1861(aa)(2) of the Act).
(iv) A Federally qualified health center (as defined in section 1861(aa)(4) of the Act).
(v) A hospital (as defined in section 1861(e) of the Act).
(vi) A hospital-based or critical access hospital-based renal dialysis center (including satellites).
(vii) A skilled nursing facility (as defined in section 1819(a) of the Act).
(viii) A community mental health center (as defined in section 1861(ff)(3)(B) of the Act).

Corporate Practice of Medicine

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Do state laws or regulations prohibit the corporate practice of medicine? Yes [6] New Hampshire expressly prohibits the corporate practice of dentistry and optometry. Language in the state's professional corporation statute could be the basis for a prohibition on other health care professions, including medicine. New Hampshire also expressly prohibits pharmacists from engaging in fee splitting.
