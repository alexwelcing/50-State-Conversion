How does the state define telemedicine?    

"Telehealth services" is a home based health monitoring system used to collect and transmit an individual's clinical data for monitoring and interpretation.[18]

Telehealth' is a mode of delivering healthcare services that utilizes information and communication technologies to enable the diagnosis, consultation, treatment, education, care management, and self management of patients at a distance from health care providers.[19]

Telemedicine is the use of an interactive telecommunications system to provide two-way, real-time, interactive communication between a provider and a Medicaid recipient across a distance.[3]

Corporate Practice of Medicine

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Do state laws or regulations prohibit the corporate practice of medicine? Yes[1] CPOM is Prohibited, but Corporations May Employ Physicians in Certain Circumstances. A business corporation may not practice medicine in South Dakota; however, a corporation in South Dakota is not engaged in the practice of medicine by entering into an employment agreement with a licensed physician if the agreement or the relationship it creates does not create a relationship that: (a) in any manner, directly or indirectly, supplants, diminishes, or regulates the physician’s independent judgment concerning the practice of medicine or the diagnosis or treatment of any patient; (b) results in profit to the corporation from the practice of medicine, such as through the corporation charging a greater fee for the physician’s services than the physician would reasonably charge as an independent contractor, except that the corporation may make additional charges reasonable associated with the services rendered such as facility, equipment, or administrative charges; or (c) remain effective for a period of more than three years, after which it may be renewed by both parties annually.
