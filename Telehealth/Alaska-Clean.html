How does the state define telemedicine?    
"Telemedicine" means the practice of health care delivery, evaluation, diagnosis, consultation, or treatment, using the transfer of medical data, audio, visual, or data communications that are performed over two or more locations between providers who are physically separated from the recipient or from each other.[1]

 

Practice Standards and Licensure

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Must a provider establish a relationship with a patient prior to engaging with them via telemedicine? No [11] A physician is not required to carry out a physical exam prior to treating or diagnosing a patient if:
(1) the physician or another provider is available to provide follow-up care; and
(2) the physician follows guidelines for obtaining patient consent and sending copies of the patient's records to the person’s primary care provider.
Must a telepresenter or health care provider be present with a patient during a telemedicine encounter? No Reference Found No mention in state Medicaid policy.
Must providers obtain informed consent from a patient prior to a telemedicine encounter? No Reference Found No mention in state Medicaid policy.
If applicable, what type of consent is required? No Reference Found No mention in state Medicaid policy.
Can telemedicine providers from bordering states legally conduct telemedicine visits with patients in this state? Yes[12] SB 74 expands the use of telehealth services and eliminates the requirement that providers rendering telemedicine services be located in Alaska.
Has this state enacted laws adopting the Federal State Medical Boards (FSMB) compact which enforces expedited licensing for out-of-state practices? No [3]

Medicaid Service Limitations

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state Medicaid program apply any geographic restrictions to telemedicine encounters? No Reference Found  
Does the state Medicaid program limit coverage and reimbursement for telemedicine to a prescribed set of health conditions or services? Yes[4][5] Conditions or services include
  • an initial visit;
  • a follow-up visit;
  • a consultation made to confirm a diagnosis;
  • a diagnostic, therapeutic, or interpretive service;
  • a psychiatric or substance abuse assessment;
  • psychotherapy;
  • or pharmacological management services on an individual recipient basis.
Several telemedicine services, are explicitly not covered by Medicaid:
  • home and community-based waiver services;
  • pharmacy services;
  • durable medical equipment services;
  • transportation services;
  • accommodation services;
  • end-stage renal disease services;
  • direct-entry midwife services;
  • private-duty nursing services;
  • personal care assistant services;
  • visual care, dispensing, or optician services"
Does the state Medicaid program limit the frequency with which they will reimburse for telemedicine services for a given patient? No Reference Found
Does the state Medicaid plan restrict or provide exceptions for services provided via telemedicine for end-stage renal disease or dialysis? (e.g., in-person check-ups between treatments) Yes[4] "The department will not pay for end-stage renal disease service provided by telemedicine application."

Medicaid-Eligible Patient Settings

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state limit Medicaid reimbursement for telemedicine services based on patient setting (e.g., where the patient is located at the time of a telemedicine encounter)? No[5]

Medicaid-Eligible Provider Type

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state limit Medicaid reimbursement for telemedicine services based on the type of provider facilitating the telemedicine encounter? Yes[6][7][8] Eligible providers include:
  • physician
  • podiatrist
  • chiropractor
  • physician assistant
  • nurse practitioner
  • licensed audiologist
  • speech-language pathologist
  • professional counselor
  • marriage and family therapist
  • psychologist
  • psychological associate
  • social worker
  • physical therapist
  • occupational therapist
  • registered speech-language
  • pathologist assistant

Medicaid-Eligible Technologies

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via live video conference? Yes[5]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via store and forward technologies? Yes[5]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via remote patient monitoring? Yes[5]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via email/phone/fax? No[5]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for synchronous telemedicine encounters? Yes[5]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for asynchronous telemedicine encounters? Yes[5]  
 

Corporate Practice of Medicine

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Do state laws or regulations prohibit the corporate practice of medicine? No[10] The corporate practice of medicine prohibition is codified in the statute stating that no corporation may operate for the purpose of providing medical services without a certificate of registration from the Arkansas State Medical Board.
