How does the state define telemedicine?    
"Telehealth" means the mode of delivering health care services and public health via information and communication technologies to facilitate the diagnosis, consultation, treatment, education, care management, and self-management of a patient's health care while the patient is at the originating site and the health care provider is at a distant site. Telehealth facilitates patient self-management and caregiver support for patients and includes synchronous interactions and asynchronous store and forward transfers. For the purposes of this subdivision, "telehealth" shall include "telemedicine" as the term is referenced in Sections 482.12, 482.22, and 485.616 of Title 42 of the Code of Federal Regulations."[1]

 

Practice Standards and Licensure

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Must a provider establish a relationship with a patient prior to engaging with them via telemedicine? No[1]
Must a telepresenter or health care provider be present with a patient during a telemedicine encounter? No Reference Found  
Must providers obtain informed consent from a patient prior to a telemedicine encounter? Yes[2]  
If applicable, what type of consent is required? Written OR Verbal[2] Consent must be documented
Can telemedicine providers from bordering states legally conduct telemedicine visits with patients in this state? No[3] No reciprocity for providers licensed in bordering states, but statute permits any provider licensed by CA and enrolled in Medi-Cal to conduct telehealth services.
Has this state enacted laws adopting the Federal State Medical Boards (FSMB) compact which enforces expedited licensing for out-of-state practices? No [4]  
 

Medicaid Coverage and Reimbursement

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Is there state legislation regarding Medicaid reimbursement for telemedicine services? Yes[1][5]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for services rendered via telemedicine? Yes[1][5] The Telemedicine Act of 1996 (SB 1665) enabled Medicaid reimbursement of telemedicine services. AB 415 was passed in 2013 and replaced the legal terminology of "telemedicine" with "telehealth," among other provisions.
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse telemedicine services at the same rate as services delivered in-person? Yes[6]  

Medicaid Service Limitations

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state Medicaid program apply any geographic restrictions to telemedicine encounters? No Reference Found  
Does the state Medicaid program limit coverage and reimbursement for telemedicine to a prescribed set of health conditions or services? Yes[3][8][9]
  • Selected Evaluation and Management (E&M) services for patient visit and consultation
  • Selected psychiatric diagnostic interview examination and selected psychiatric therapeutic services
  • Teledermatology by store and forward
  • Teleophthalmology by store and forward
  • Teledentistry
  • Transmission costs (up to 90 minutes per patient, per day, per provider)
  • Originating site facility fee
  • Interpretation and report of X-rays and electrocardiograms performed via telehealth
  • Speech therapy services under the Local Educational Agency (LEA) Medi-Cal Billing Option Program
Does the state Medicaid program limit the frequency with which they will reimburse for telemedicine services for a given patient? No Reference Found "Medicaid limits its coverage of interactive electronic consultations to two (2) per client per year in a physician’s office or a hospital. This yearly limit is based on the State Fiscal Year (July 1 through June 30). The Program approves benefit extension requests for clients of all ages, based on documented medical necessity. "
Does the state Medicaid plan restrict or provide exceptions for services provided via telemedicine for end-stage renal disease or dialysis? (e.g., in-person check-ups between treatments) No Reference Found  

Medicaid-Eligible Patient Settings

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state limit Medicaid reimbursement for telemedicine services based on patient setting (e.g., where the patient is located at the time of a telemedicine encounter)? No[1]

Medicaid-Eligible Provider Type

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state limit Medicaid reimbursement for telemedicine services based on the type of provider facilitating the telemedicine encounter? No [3] The state does not limit reimbursement to specific provider types, but the provider performing services via telemedicine must be licensed in CA and enrolled as a Medi-Cal provider.

Medicaid-Eligible Technologies

Question Answer Clarifying Information
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via live video conference? Yes[3]
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via store and forward technologies? Yes[7] Medi-Cal will reimburse for store and forward, asynchronous transmission of medical information for teleophthalmology, teledermatology, and teledentistry.
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via remote patient monitoring? No Reference Found  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for telemedicine services provided via email/phone/fax? No [3]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for sychronous telemedicine encounters? Yes[3]  
Does the state Medicaid program reimburse for asychronous telemedicine encounters? Yes[7] Medi-Cal will reimburse for store and forward, asynchronous transmission of medical information for teleophthalmology, teledermatology, and teledentistry.
